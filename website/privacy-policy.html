1. Overview

This Privacy Policy governs the collection, storage, use, and sharing of personal information by TomCodeX Inc ("Company", "we", "us", "our") through its website tomcodex.com and products including SentinelFlow and VertexDB.

This policy is published in compliance with:

  • Information Technology Act, 2000 IT Act 2000
  • IT (Reasonable Security Practices and Procedures and SPDI) Rules, 2011 SPDI Rules 2011
  • Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 DPDP Act 2023
  • IT (Intermediary Guidelines and Digital Media Ethics Code) Rules, 2021 IT Rules 2021

By using our website or services, you consent to the collection and use of your information as described in this policy. If you do not agree, please discontinue use of our services.

2. Data We Collect

2.1 Information You Provide Directly

  • Full name and email address (via contact or demo forms)
  • Company name and job title
  • Phone number (if provided voluntarily)
  • Messages and communications you send us

2.2 Automatically Collected Information

  • IP address and approximate geographic location
  • Browser type, operating system, and device information
  • Pages visited, time spent, and referral URLs
  • Cookies and similar tracking technologies

2.3 Salesforce Integration Data

When using SentinelFlow within a Salesforce environment, we may process metadata related to integration logs, incident records, and system telemetry. This data is processed solely within the customer's Salesforce org and is governed by the customer's own data policies and the applicable Salesforce agreements.

3. Sensitive Personal Data or Information (SPDI)

As defined under Rule 3 of the SPDI Rules 2011 SPDI Rules 2011, we do not intentionally collect the following categories of sensitive personal data:

  • Passwords (stored only in hashed/encrypted form)
  • Financial information (bank account, credit/debit card numbers)
  • Physical, physiological, or mental health condition data
  • Sexual orientation
  • Medical records or history
  • Biometric information

If any such information is inadvertently provided, it will be deleted promptly and not processed.

4. Purpose of Collection

We collect personal data only for the following lawful purposes DPDP Act 2023 §4:

  • To respond to inquiries, demo requests, and support tickets
  • To provide, maintain, and improve our products and services
  • To send product updates, security notices, and service communications
  • To comply with legal obligations under Indian law
  • To detect and prevent fraud, abuse, or security threats
  • To analyze usage patterns and improve user experience

We do not use your personal data for automated profiling that produces legal or significant effects without your explicit consent.

5. Sharing of Data

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information. We may share data in the following limited circumstances:

5.1 Service Providers

Trusted third-party processors (e.g., hosting, email delivery, analytics) who process data on our behalf under contractual data processing agreements.

5.2 Legal Requirements

When required by law, court order, or governmental authority in India under the IT Act 2000 or any applicable legislation IT Act §69, §69B.

5.3 Business Transfers

In the event of a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets, personal data may be transferred to the acquiring entity, subject to equivalent privacy protections.

We do not disclose personal data to any third party without your consent except as described above or where required by applicable Indian law.

6. Data Retention

We retain personal data only for as long as necessary to fulfil the purpose for which it was collected DPDP Act 2023 §8(7):

  • Contact form submissions: 24 months from submission date
  • Trial and demo inquiry data: 12 months from last interaction
  • Transaction records: 7 years (as required under applicable Indian tax laws)
  • Support tickets: 36 months from resolution

Upon expiry of the retention period, data is securely erased or anonymised.

7. Security Practices

We implement reasonable security practices and procedures as mandated under Rule 8 of the SPDI Rules 2011 SPDI Rules 2011 §8, including:

  • Encryption of data in transit using TLS 1.2+
  • Access control and role-based permissions for internal systems
  • Regular security assessments and vulnerability reviews
  • Secure credential storage using industry-standard hashing

While we take all reasonable precautions, no method of electronic transmission or storage is 100% secure. In the event of a personal data breach, we will notify affected users as required under applicable law.

8. Your Rights Under the DPDP Act 2023

As a Data Principal under the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 DPDP Act 2023, you have the following rights:

  • Right to Access — Obtain confirmation of whether your personal data is being processed and a summary of such data (§11)
  • Right to Correction — Request correction of inaccurate or misleading personal data (§12)
  • Right to Erasure — Request erasure of personal data that is no longer necessary (§12)
  • Right to Grievance Redressal — Lodge a complaint with our Grievance Officer (§13)
  • Right to Nominate — Nominate another individual to exercise your rights in the event of death or incapacity (§14)

To exercise any of these rights, contact our Grievance Officer at the details provided in Section 12.

9. Cookies and Tracking

Our website uses cookies to enhance your browsing experience. Types of cookies used:

  • Strictly Necessary — Essential for basic website functionality (cannot be disabled)
  • Analytics — Help us understand how visitors interact with the site (e.g., page views, session duration)
  • Preference — Store your settings and preferences

You may disable non-essential cookies through your browser settings. Doing so may affect certain website features.

10. Third-Party Services

Our website may contain links to third-party websites or integrate with services such as Salesforce, Google, and others. This Privacy Policy does not govern third-party data practices. We encourage you to review the privacy policies of any third-party services you interact with.

Our Salesforce integration is subject to Salesforce's own Privacy Policy and Data Processing Addendum as executed between Salesforce and the customer entity.

11. Children's Privacy

Our services are not directed at individuals under the age of 18. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children. If you believe a minor has provided us with personal information, please contact our Grievance Officer immediately and we will take steps to delete such information DPDP Act 2023 §9.

Continued use of our website or services after the effective date of any changes constitutes your acceptance of the updated policy.
